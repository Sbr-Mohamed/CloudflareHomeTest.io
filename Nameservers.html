Thank you for reaching out to us about Cloudflare's nameservers and how they compare to DNS
services like Google DNS or OpenDNS. I'll be glad to explain the differences and address your
questions.

Cloudflare's nameservers offer several distinct advantages:

  1. Security and Performance:Cloudflare operates one of the largest, most robust networks in the
    world, which means our DNS service benefits from global coverage and built-in security features
    like DDoS protection and DNSSEC (Domain Name System Security Extensions). This ensures that
    your DNS queries are resolved quickly and securely.
  2. Privacy:Cloudflare is committed to privacy. Unlike some other DNS providers, we do not log
    your IP address for more than 24 hours and do not sell your data to advertisers.
  3. Integrated Services:By using Cloudflare's nameservers, you can easily integrate with other
    Cloudflare services such as content delivery network (CDN) capabilities, DDoS protection, and
    web application firewall (WAF) services. This integration can help improve the performance and
    security of your website.

Regarding your question about delegating to Cloudflare's nameservers while still using your host's
DNS, yes, it is possible. You can delegate specific subdomains to Cloudflare's nameservers while
keeping other DNS records managed by your hosting provider. This can be done by setting up
appropriate DNS records (like NS records) with your current DNS provider.

If you need assistance with the setup or have more questions about integrating Cloudflare DNS with
your existing setup, feel free to provide more details or reach out to our support team. We're here to
help!
